Identity, Values & Strengths.

24 March 2024


Who am I?

If I’m honest, not too long ago this question would have completely rattled me, my internal dialogue would have been uncertain and most likely defensive. But now I feel comfortable answering who, and why, I am who I am. Maybe that’s growth? I’m not sure but I’ll claim it.

I’ve been extremely lucky to be surrounded by great people throughout my life, and by that, I mean my family to who I am very close to. We lost dad about a year ago, which was the worst. He was such a legend, my biggest supporter, biggest critic and best mate. But we were very lucky to have him.

I learnt from dad always to be nice to people, back yourself and not be afraid to have a punt (both literally and metaphorically). I really value people and the relationships I hold and am very empathetic. I feel as though I got that from the old fella.

My mum is also a legend, she taught me the value of hard work and balance in life. Whatever you want to achieve you can achieve. I mean, I think her story is really inspiring – from a young single mum, mental health nurse to CEO. Stick to your guns and be sure that whatever you want to do or are doing aligns with your values.

Strength wise, I’m positive, adaptable, curious and resilient. I believe these qualities will assist me on my journey at Dev Academy. Especially when things become difficult.

A potential limitation of my learning and career development. When I was out for a run the other morning and thought to myself that growing up, I got a lot of praise, and as an adult I’ve struggled at times when praise hasn’t been easy to come by. This has led me to be hypercritical of myself. I realise now that I need to be clear on my own definition of success rather than relying on the approval of others.

I can also be hyper obsessive regarding things, a good example of this is exercise. I can’t just get into ‘shape’ I need to be the fittest, strongest, healthiest I’ve ever been. This means I’m highly regimented with routine and diet. Sometimes going to the gym twice a day. I think I strive for perfection. I identify that this could be a potential limitation on my learning as it directly transfers to what I’m doing now - potentially spending hours on something that could take 10min to make sure it looks perfect.

As a cricket coach I am often met with challenges surrounding productivity and making difficult decisions. I like to believe that all decisions I make are aligned to mine and whoever I’m working withs values, be it a team or an individual. If you are open, adaptable and can seek understanding in a non-confrontational way, people can progress forward in harmony.
